# Omarchy Anthony J. Russell Theme

A monochrome Omarchy dark theme inspired by Andrew J. Russell's photographs documenting the construction of America's first transcontinental railroad.

![GitHub stars](https://img.shields.io/github/stars/mattbbia/railroad-visions-omarchy?style=flat-square) ![License](https://img.shields.io/github/license/mattbbia/railroad-visions-omarchy?style=flat-square)

![Preview Image](preview.png)

## Inspiration

Every project is its own railroad. Some days the track stretches effortlessly across open ground. Other days progress stops at a collapsed bridge, a derailed train, or a mountain that leaves no way forward but through. These photographs serve as a reminder that meaningful work is rarely a straight line. Progress comes in setbacks, persistence, and eventually, completion.

The wallpapers are drawn from the work of Andrew J. Russell, one of the earliest photographers to document the construction of the first transcontinental railroad in the United States. His images capture both the engineering achievements and the obstacles faced along the way, making them a fitting backdrop for the long, often uneven process of building projects of our own.

## Installation

Clone the repository into your Omarchy themes directory:

``` bash
omarchy-theme-install https://github.com/mattbbia/railroad-visions-omarchy.git
```
```
```
### OR

1. Open the Omarchy menu (**Super + Alt + Space**).
2. Go to **Install > Style > Theme**.
3. Paste this repo URL: `https://github.com/mattbbia/railroad-visions-omarchy.git`
4. Hit Enter.
